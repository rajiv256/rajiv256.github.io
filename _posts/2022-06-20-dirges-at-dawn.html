Went running in the woods at dawn; listening to

the songs of a bird and sonnets of the Bard;

As the wind whistled past, a memory of her glistened;

Many years ago, in the maycounty fair;

We held hands and heralded fortunes,

Shattered our prisons and chartered new courses;

Sadly, however, we hesitated to accost,

For the dream came at a cost;

It’s all a faze though I could never forget her face;

Many years later, far away from the fair, 

saw her from afar;

Moist eyes, dry smile, and glazed cheeks;

Maybe I was right in not confronting,

It was in the past, so I can do all the contorting;

I watched the fog rising through the trees

scorched in all colors black;

A little shrub rose, from a trunk chopped to the brink;

In that vein, maybe, it was all not in vain?!
