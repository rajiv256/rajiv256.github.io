And as the bus whirred,

the bovine retreated making way,

bringing my thoughts from far away,

leaving my insides stirred.

It hurled through sand-crusted roads,

greeted by the hooded plateaus,

and the screechings of mayqueens.

For in a lonely seat I sat and wept,

unnoticed by the passing trees,

or the passengers fast asleep.

For the broken bridges,

for life's ridges.

People say, "the oysters are rife",

but, when all the riches can't get you,

Pearls, why I should fare?

And when the curtain falls,

Why can't I despair?
