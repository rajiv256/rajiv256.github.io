# My Two Cents

*2026-06-17*

> Happiness may lie in how you spent the day, but true happiness is in
> what you come home to.

I saw a meme today on r/ProgrammerHumor as to how executives are
regretting firing those employees as the Claude subscription costs are
ginormous. It felt a little silly. I think a more pointed and fairer
question to ask is – how many software engineer hours did Claude’s one
month subscription save?

*2026-06-20*

What is life? is a question that puzzles philosophers and scientists
alike. Famously, Schrodinger in his series of talks has elucidated what
life does but couldn’t really tell what life is. A widely accepted
definition goes something like–any thing that has some “common
properties” such as autonomy, homeostasis, metabolism, growth,
adaptation, response to stimuli, and reproduction is considered to be
alive. But this definition is not without its problems. Let’s examine
one of these properties which I think is a bit vague–autonomy. Darwin
has given us the idea of “survival of the fittest”. This inherently
assumes that all life strives to survive and whoever strives the best
and whoever’s environment allows it survives. But where does this
“strive” to survive come from? Is it hard coded into DNA? Is it a
consequence of the laws of physics? Or is it something else? For
example, a fire looks for resources, tries to expand, adapts to stimuli.
Is fire alive? Many such questions come up. Another example, is a virus
alive?
