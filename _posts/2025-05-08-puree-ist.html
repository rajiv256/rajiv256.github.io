I didn't read much of modern prose/poetry, but most of what I have read has been pretty bland and uninspired. Including the ones I wrote. Granted, I only started acquiring vocabulary AFTER I failed an English exam at the beginning of my undergrad. I get a free-pass. I'd like to think it was this desire to be better that made me spend all that time watching countless re-runs of Friends, Office, and The West Wing. Especially the last one.

Often, classical literature tends to reach to my heart strings and puts a Windsor knot on them. Modern prose never came close. (such rhyme. I am not half bad)

I was recently reading some quotes of Ray Bradbury, who by the way shares my admonition of modern writing said, "If you step on a live mine, make it your own. Be blown up, as it were, by your own delights and despairs." Damn, how beautiful was that. And he said it in an interview, just out of nowhere.

I then went hunting for more such phrases, and as it were, I found this one, "Stuff your eyes with wonder, live as if you'd drop dead in ten seconds." Such a platitude, but more than words, it is the expression that makes it great, I think.
