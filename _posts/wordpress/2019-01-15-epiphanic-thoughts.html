Robins coming out of their nests, 
Batmans going into hiding
Sun’s not up yet, 
Mist hasn’t settled yet. 

A paperwallah, driving through a dew-covered road
Whistling his favorite melody, no-one to judge
Greeted by an occasional team of stray dogs
That gave way for their well-known stranger!   

An occasional tea shop that served sparkling teas
Featured monkey capped men in their forties,
Remembering their forgotten dreams and lost opportunities
Searching for their one moment of rejoice.    

A just awoken homeless guy on the pavement
His body covered from head to hip by a jute blanket,
His eyes gazing the road with a known uncertainty
Contemplating the past or worrying about future  

As I bicycled through the misty air on a secluded morning
I felt so little and so big at the same time,
watching all these

That's when it hit me,
For mankind,
there should be moments
No rules, No worries, No pressures and No deadlines

So I closed my eyes and turned my face up to the sky, 
And stretched my hands outwards, still pedalling
Unaware of the traffic signal that passed by
Because now in my world,
there is only Me and no one else,
And I was free.
