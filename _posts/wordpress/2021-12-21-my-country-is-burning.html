My country is burning

My country is burning

From the heart of my nation

Hatred is effusing

My brothers are fleeing

With hearts in their fists

My sisters are fleeing

With children at their breasts

From the heart

My country is bleeding

How will you understand?

That when poverty runs amok

That when Money, Lies and Secret agendas storm the nation's fabric

The same comrades that were mobilised for rallies

Can also be mobilised for killings

Whom should I tell?

If the very person who assured to protect me

Molests me

The very person who promised to foster me

Abuses me

Should I blame the society?

Who wants things to be hunky-dory

And refuses to oppose the naked assault

Because they are privileged enough

To sit in the comfort of their own homes

Heeding a blind-eye and deaf-ear

To my brothers' burning houses

And their scrambling voices

Or should I abuse the educated illiterates?

Who soar in the painless drudgery

Of fabricated lies and fake news

Sitting on a fence watching and laughing

At my brothers and sisters being oppressed

My country is burning

My country is burning

Yes, I am helpless and numb

I feel compassion for my oppressed brothers,

But then, I am an Urban Naxal

I feel angry towards my leaders

But then, I am an anti-national

I can see through the lies I've been told

But calling them out is, Deshdroh

When I do raise my voice

It's a disturbance for my neighbours,

They say, I am causing ruckus

They say I am a sold-out and a whore

But I stand tall and fearless

With my anxious heart beating under my Burkha

For still there are brothers who care for me

They are looking up to me

Cheering me and Jeering me on

Egging me to win my battles on my own

It's not late still, my friends

There's still time

The houses might've burnt down

The roofs might've beaten down

But as long as we have love and compassion in our hearts

The foundations remain taut

The fabric of our nation remains unburnt

Yes, the country is burning

But the ideas for which it's people stand for

The idea of oneness

The idea of unity in diversity

The ideas of compassion and brotherhood

The idea of conquering hatred with love

The idea of conquering oppression with peace

The idea of tolerance and equality

These can neither be destroyed nor be vandalised

They stand steadfast in the face of adversity

Because you can't kill an idea that is etched into the minds of a billion people

You can't kill the ideas our founding-fathers dreamt for us as a nation

As a great person once said,

దేశమంటే మట్టి కాదోయ్, దేశమంటే మనుషులోయ్
(A nation isn't made of dirt
A nation is made by its people!)
